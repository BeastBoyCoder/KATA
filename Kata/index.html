Shotokan Karate all Katas

Kata is a Japanese word that means "form". It is a structured way of doing things or practice. In Japanese martial arts, kata is a detailed choreographed pattern of movements that are practiced alone or in groups. It is a way to memorize and perfect the movements being executed. In karate, kata is the practice of performing sequences of specific moves targeting a virtual opponent. Athletes must perform a different kata each time. Technical and athletic performance are given separate scores using the same scale from 5.0 to 10.0. Kata can also be a prefix meaning "down," "against," or "back". It occurs originally in loanwords from Greek, such as "cataclysm," "catalog," and "catalepsy".
